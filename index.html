Finding Community in Faith: My Journey to Riverview Church

March 15, 2025 By Jessica Matthews

When I first moved to Perth last year, I felt lost in a sea of unfamiliar faces. Moving across the country meant leaving behind my support network of family and friends. I found myself in a beautiful city, but without the connections that make a place feel like home.

For the first few months, I focused on settling into my new job and apartment. Weekends were spent exploring local cafes and parks, but something was missing. I craved meaningful connections and a sense of belonging that seemed just out of reach.

A Warm Welcome

One Sunday morning, at the recommendation of a colleague, I decided to visit Riverview Church. I remember feeling nervous as I approached the building, not knowing what to expect. Would I feel out of place? Would people notice I was new but keep their distance?

Those worries melted away almost immediately. Before I even reached the main entrance, a friendly greeter welcomed me with a warm smile. "First time at Riverview?" she asked. When I nodded, she offered to show me around and introduced me to a few regular attendees who were my age.

More Than Just Sunday Service

What started as a casual Sunday visit soon became the highlight of my week. The worship was uplifting, and the messages were relevant to challenges I was facing in my own life. But what truly surprised me was how quickly I found myself becoming part of the community.

After a few weeks, I joined a small group that meets on Wednesday evenings. We discuss the Sunday message, share our experiences, and support each other through life's ups and downs. These weekly gatherings have become a safe space where I can be vulnerable and authentic.

Finding Purpose Through Service

As I became more involved, I discovered opportunities to serve that aligned with my skills and interests. Now I volunteer with the youth ministry twice a month, helping teenagers navigate the same questions about faith and identity that I once struggled with.

There's something powerful about being part of something bigger than yourself. The sense of purpose I've found through serving others has added a dimension to my life that I didn't even know was missing.

A Place to Call Home

It's been a year since my first visit to Riverview Church, and what a transformative year it's been. I've found not just a place to worship on Sundays, but a community that feels like family. The friendships I've formed extend beyond church walls‚Äîwe celebrate birthdays together, help each other move apartments, and show up for one another during difficult times.

If you're new to the area or simply searching for community, I can't recommend Riverview enough. Whether you're a lifelong churchgoer or someone who's curious but hesitant, you'll find a place where you are welcomed exactly as you are.

Sometimes finding home isn't about a physical location‚Äîit's about finding people who make you feel like you belong. That's what Riverview has been for me, and I'm grateful every day for the community I've found here.
